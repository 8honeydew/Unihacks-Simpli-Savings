Why should we save electricity?

When we consume less power, we reduce the amount of toxic fumes released by power plants, and conserve non-renewable sources. To generate electricity, most power plants use coal, crude, or other fossil fuels. Although this method of creating energy is inexpensive and effective, our planet pays the price. Burning fossil fuels releases harmful greenhouse gasses such as carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, etc. Greenhouse gasses absorb the sun’s warmth and trap heat onto the earth's surface. However with the exponential emission of greenhouse gasses, the earth traps more and more heat, increasing the temperature, contributing to global warming. This in turn leads to rising temperatures, heat waves, higher sea levels due to ice melting, abnormal weather patterns, smog, acid rain, and habitat loss of many species. When we use less electricity there is a less demand for energy production which in turn creates less demand for harvesting fossil fuels, contributing to lower emissions of greenhouse gasses.

    How to Save electricty?


  • Using LED lights: LED lights use 75-80% less energy than traditional 60-watt bulbs.
  • Use natural light during the day
  • Turn off lights when not in use
  • Use a programmable or smart thermostat which uses AI to adjust inside temperature based on the weather outside
  • Install energy efficient windows to prevent heat loss through them.
  • Take shorter showers with hot water
  • Unplug unused electronics
